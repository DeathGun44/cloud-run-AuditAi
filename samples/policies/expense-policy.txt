COMPANY EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT POLICY

Effective Date: January 1, 2025

1. GENERAL GUIDELINES
   - All business expenses must be reasonable, necessary, and directly related to company business
   - Original itemized receipts are required for all expenses over $25
   - Expenses must be submitted within 30 days of occurrence
   - Personal expenses are not reimbursable

2. MEAL EXPENSES
   - Business meals: Up to $75 per person for dinner, $50 for lunch, $25 for breakfast
   - Team meals require manager pre-approval
   - Alcohol is NOT reimbursable under any circumstances
   - Tips should not exceed 20% of the meal cost

3. TRANSPORTATION
   - Taxi/Rideshare: Reimbursable for business travel when public transit is impractical
   - Personal vehicle mileage: $0.67 per mile (standard IRS rate)
   - Parking: Reimbursable with receipt
   - Traffic violations and parking tickets: NOT reimbursable

4. LODGING
   - Hotel stays: Up to $200 per night in major cities, $150 elsewhere
   - Must book reasonable accommodations; luxury hotels require VP approval
   - Personal entertainment charges (movies, minibar, spa): NOT reimbursable

5. OFFICE SUPPLIES & EQUIPMENT
   - Office supplies under $100: No pre-approval needed
   - Equipment over $500: Requires manager approval before purchase
   - Software subscriptions: Must be approved by IT department

6. PROHIBITED EXPENSES
   - Alcoholic beverages
   - Personal entertainment
   - Gifts without prior approval
   - Fines, penalties, or legal fees
   - Political contributions

7. RECEIPT REQUIREMENTS
   - Must be legible and show:
     * Date of transaction
     * Merchant name and location
     * Itemized list of goods/services
     * Total amount paid
   - Digital receipts and photos are acceptable
   - Credit card statements alone are NOT sufficient

8. APPROVAL WORKFLOW
   - Expenses under $500: Manager approval
   - Expenses $500-$2000: Director approval
   - Expenses over $2000: VP or CFO approval

For questions, contact finance@company.com
